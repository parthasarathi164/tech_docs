# Project Synopsis: ARP4754A Compliant Design of an Oleo-Pneumatic Strut for Commercial Aircraft

## 1. Objectives of the Work

The primary goal of this project is to develop a robust design and verification framework for a main landing gear oleo-pneumatic shock strut. By following the **ARP4754A** standard, the project ensures that the development process is requirement-driven and safety-oriented.

The key objectives are:

- **Functional Requirement Capture:** To define the operational boundaries and performance criteria for a commercial-class rear landing gear system.
    
- **Development Assurance:** To assign and meet appropriate **Development Assurance Levels (DAL)** based on the criticality of the landing gear function.
    
- **Energy Absorption Design:** To design a dual-chamber system (oil and gas) that provides efficient damping and static support.
    
- **Safety Integration:** To identify potential failure modes and implement design mitigations early in the development cycle.
    
- **Mathematical Modeling:** To establish the fundamental governing physics of the strut:
    
    $$F_{total} = F_{gas} + F_{damping} + F_{friction}$$
    

---

## 2. Current Methodology (The V-Model Approach)

This project utilizes the **V-Model** to bridge the gap between abstract requirements and a verified physical design.

### Phase 1: Requirements & Functional Analysis (Left Side)

- **System-Level Requirements:** Establishing what the strut must do (e.g., absorb impact, support ground weight, provide steering/braking interfaces).
    
- **Safety Assessment:** Performing a **Functional Hazard Assessment (FHA)** to determine the effects of system failures on the aircraft.
    
- **Preliminary Architecture:** Defining the physical layout of the inner and outer cylinders, orifice plates, and nitrogen chambers.
    

### Phase 2: Implementation & Detailed Design (The Bottom)

- **3D CAD Development:** Creating a high-fidelity assembly that accounts for spatial constraints and structural load paths.
    
- **Material Research:** Evaluating aerospace-grade alloys for high-strength, fatigue-resistant performance.
    

### Phase 3: Verification & Validation (Right Side)

- **Verification:** Using simulation tools (FEA and CFD) to ensure the design meets the derived requirements.
    
- **Validation:** Confirming that the final design satisfies the original stakeholder needs through virtual drop tests and performance analysis.
    

---

## 3. Expected Outcomes

- **Traceable Requirement Set:** A comprehensive document showing the flow-down from high-level aircraft needs to specific component dimensions.
    
- **Optimized Design Assembly:** A complete 3D model of the oleo-pneumatic strut including internal damping components.
    
- **Safety Documentation:** A **Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA)** report highlighting how the design handles potential malfunctions.
    
- **Verification Reports:** Data-driven evidence proving the strut can handle predicted operational loads and environmental conditions.
    

---

## 4. Proof of Concept (POC)

The Proof of Concept for this proposal phase acts as a **Methodological Validation**. We will demonstrate that our analytical models are capable of predicting real-world behavior before physical resources are committed.

The POC will consist of:

- **A Functional Logic Model:** Showing how the nitrogen spring and hydraulic damping interact to dissipate energy.
    
- **Load Simulation Strategy:** A plan for a "Virtual Drop Test" that will be used to verify that the stroke efficiency meets industry standards.
    
- **Safety Margin Framework:** A demonstration of how the design will maintain structural integrity even under degraded conditions (e.g., partial pressure loss).
