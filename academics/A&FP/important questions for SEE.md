# Unique Part-B questions (deduplicated)

1. Derive an expression for the **classical thin-airfoil theory** (considering elementary flows).
    
2. Derive an equation for the **strength of a vortex sheet** placed along the chord of an airfoil and show how the Fourier coefficients (e.g. ($A_0, A_1$)) depend on angle of attack and camber.
    
3. Explain the **Kutta condition** (why it’s critical for steady flow around an airfoil) and show / prove related results about vorticity at different trailing-edge shapes.
    
4. Describe **downwash** and its impact on finite wings, including its role in generating **induced drag** and its relation to spanwise lift distribution.
    
5. Derive the **Biot–Savart law** and **Helmholtz theorems** in the aerodynamic context and explain their application to vortex filaments around finite wings.
    
6. Derive **Prandtl’s lifting-line theory** (fundamental equation), and discuss the assumptions and limitations.
    
7. Explain the concept and significance of a **semi-infinite vortex filament** in aerodynamics.
    
8. Derive the **velocity potential equation** for an inviscid, compressible, subsonic flow over a body immersed in a uniform stream.
    
9. Derive an expression for the **linearized pressure coefficient** for small perturbations in the flow.
    
10. Sketch the variation of **profile drag coefficient** with freestream Mach number and explain the concept of **drag-divergence Mach number**.
    
11. Explain the **critical Mach number**, and show graphically the effect of airfoil thickness on critical Mach number (design implications).
    
12. Derive the **general expression for endurance (E)** of a propeller-driven airplane.
    
13. **Define** and give mathematical expressions for: (i) Time to climb, (ii) Maximum climb angle ($\theta_{\max}$), (iii) Rate of climb, (iv) Power required and power available.
    
14. **Derive the Breguet range equation** (including the form for jet-propelled airplanes).
    
15. (Numerical) For a specified airplane (Gulfstream IV) and given data, **calculate the thrust-required curve** at a given altitude (numerical performance problem).
    
16. Derive the **equation for thrust required** (for unaccelerated steady level flight) and explain the terms / factors affecting thrust required.
    
17. Graphically explain the **thrust-required curve ($T_R$)** vs free-stream velocity ($V_\infty$).
    
18. Obtain an expression for **power required** for unaccelerated steady level flight (and discuss power-required vs power-available behavior).
    
19. For a propeller-driven aircraft, explain **thrust available** and the **maximum velocity** obtained (expressions and plot).
    
20. **Analyze jet engine power curves** and determine conditions under which a jet reaches its maximum velocity in level flight.
    
21. Derive the **Breguet range** (already listed above — kept for completeness with other phrasing).
    
22. **Define absolute and service ceilings** and derive the equations for them.
    
23. Give a brief note on **wing loading** (definition, significance).
    
24. Explain factors that influence **glide ratio** and how glide ratio affects range and endurance.
    
25. **With neat sketch, explain the sequence of events during take-off** of an airplane from a runway.
    
26. Derive the **equation for load factor** and **bank angle required** for level coordinated turns (load-factor and bank angle relation).
    
27. Derive the **equation for accelerated rate of climb**, considering thrust-to-weight ratio, drag, and specific excess power.
    
28. Derive an expression to **calculate the approach (landing) distance** required for an airplane during landing (including deceleration factors).
    
29. List and derive **factors influencing landing distance and approach speed**, and explain significance of approach speed in landing operations.
    
30. Explain the **principles of construction of a V–n diagram**, list constraints considered, and describe its significance.
    
31. Derive the **equation for load factor in level turning flight** (sometimes presented as the same as item 26—kept as single concept).
